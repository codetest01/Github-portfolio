# Prompt: Deep Product Review & Current User Issues Analysis for Any Mobile App

Act as an expert Product Manager with 15+ years of experience in digital products, mobile apps, user experience, product strategy, app growth, release management, and consumer-scale platforms.

Your task is to conduct a comprehensive and evidence-backed review of the mobile app: **[INSERT APP NAME]**.

The objective is to identify the most relevant and currently active user problems affecting the app experience, based on recent and credible user feedback from across the web.

---

# Research Scope

Analyze:

* Android app
* iOS app (if applicable)
* Mobile app experience only
* Focus on active user pain points and product gaps

---

# Source Requirements (Mandatory)

Use only credible and large-scale public feedback platforms such as:

* Google Play Store
* Apple App Store
* Reddit
* Official forums
* Product discussion communities
* Trusted tech publications
* Public issue trackers (if available)

Avoid:

* Anonymous blogs
* Low credibility forums
* Promotional articles
* AI-generated summaries without source validation

---

# Review Timeframe

Only consider:

* Feedback from the **last 6 months**
* Recently active complaints
* Issues still unresolved

If:

* A recent app update solved a previously known issue
* Recent reviews indicate improvement

→ REMOVE that issue from the final list.

Do not include historical problems that are no longer relevant.

---

# User Credibility Filter

Prioritize:

* Reddit accounts active for at least ~6 months
* Reviews with detailed descriptions
* Repeated complaints across multiple users/platforms
* Highly upvoted or frequently discussed complaints

Ignore:

* One-line emotional reviews without context
* Spam reviews
* Non-specific complaints

---

# Deliverable Objective

Identify:

1. The top current user problems
2. Recurring UX or product patterns
3. Root causes behind the issues
4. Business impact on retention, trust, engagement, or churn
5. Approximate scale of affected users

---

# Required Problem Statement Structure

For every issue, use this exact structure:

> **[User type]** face **[specific problem]** when **[context/situation]**, which leads to **[negative outcome or frustration].**

---

# Mandatory 5-Part Validation Checklist

Every problem statement must clearly answer:

1. Who is facing the problem?
2. What exactly is the problem?
3. When or where does it occur?
4. Why is it painful or frustrating?
5. What is the impact on user behavior?

If any part is missing:

* Rewrite the problem statement.

---

# Output Format

For EACH problem, provide:

---

## Problem X: [Short Title]

### Problem Statement

(Use exact structure provided above)

---

### Estimated Affected Users

Provide an approximate range:

* Example: ~5,000–20,000 users

Estimate using:

* Volume of reviews
* Frequency of mentions
* Reddit engagement
* Cross-platform recurrence

Mention clearly:

> “This is an estimated directional range based on publicly visible discussions and review patterns.”

---

### 5-Part Breakdown

* Who:
* What:
* When/Where:
* Why painful:
* Impact:

---

### Evidence & Sources

Provide:

* Direct source links
* Latest references only
* Mention month/year if available

---

### Review Snapshot

Include 1–2 short real user quotes.

Format:
⭐☆☆☆☆
“[user feedback]”

Keep quotes short and representative.

---

### Common Pattern

Identify the recurring pattern behind the issue.

Examples:

* Release regression
* Weak QA
* Poor onboarding
* Monetization confusion
* Device fragmentation
* Performance instability
* UX inconsistency
* Trust erosion

---

# Important Filtering Rules

Remove problems if:

* Recent releases solved them
* Positive reviews outweigh complaints recently
* Issue no longer appears repeatedly in last 6 months

Prioritize:

* Current active pain points
* High-frequency complaints
* High-severity issues
* Problems affecting retention/trust

---

# Final Analysis Section (Mandatory)

After listing all problems, provide:

---

## Cross-Problem Insights

### 1. Common Product Themes

Identify recurring themes across all issues.

Examples:

* Reliability issues
* Monetization dissatisfaction
* Poor release management
* Discovery friction
* Trust breakdown

---

### 2. Root Cause Hypothesis

Explain what organizational or product gaps may be causing these issues.

Examples:

* Weak QA
* Lack of phased rollouts
* Poor experimentation control
* Insufficient device testing
* UX inconsistency across teams

---

### 3. Product Management Prioritization

Create a table:

| Problem | Scale | Severity | Priority |
| ------- | ----- | -------- | -------- |

Use:

* P0 = Critical
* P1 = Important
* P2 = Medium priority

---

### 4. PM Recommendations

Provide:

* Immediate fixes (0–3 months)
* Medium-term improvements (3–6 months)
* Long-term strategic improvements (6–12 months)

---

# Writing Style Requirements

* Use concise PM-style language
* Be analytical, not emotional
* Focus on evidence-backed reasoning
* Avoid generic statements
* Avoid fluff
* Make it presentation/document ready
* Structure cleanly with headings and spacing

---

# Final Goal

The final output should feel like:

* A real product review prepared by a senior PM
* A consulting-style product diagnosis
* A submission-ready analysis for business school, product case study, or UX research project.
